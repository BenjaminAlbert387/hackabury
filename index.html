Before Hackabury Begins

What are the steps to register and participate?

  • Students need to complete the registration form by 5pm on 28th April (register here!)
  • They will then be emailed further joining instructions.
  • Students can also join the Discord to chat in advance of the event.

What are the themes or challenges?

  • You will find that out on the day.
  • But if you join the Discord...
  • The Hackabury Discord Server.
  • We will reveal who the sponsors are throughout the coming weeks!

What is the structure and format of the event?

  • Students will arrive at 9.30am for registration, with a 10am start time.
  • Industry Sponsors will give a short presentation of their challenges.
  • Students then have 24hrs to respond to the prompt idea, in teams, using whatever methods they choose.
  • The event is designed to be fun and engaging, with students able to flex their creativity and learn new skills along the way.
  • On the second day, hacking will stop around lunchtime, and we will all come together for presentations of the projects made.
  • Judging will take place, and the winners and prizes will be announced!

Are there any prerequisites or requirements for joining?

  • It would be helpful to get a team together, but don't worry if you come as an individual.
  • We can help teams form on the first day!
  • You do not have to be an experienced coder, this event is aimed at everyone!

What should I bring on the day?

  • A laptop - it's a Bring Your Own Device event!
  • A phone charger cable and battery pack, in case you need to charge your phone.
  • Some snacks and drinks - you'll be working from 10am Tue until 5pm Wed (you can go home in between!)
  • Remember that you are responsible for keeping your personal belongings safe!
  • Get a team together, best to sign up with a team in mind, but you can also sign up as an individual.
  • Your best ideas and all your amazing tech talent!

During Hackabury

Will we be fed?

  • There will be food provided on both days.
  • We will serve pizza in the SU at 5pm on the Tue, you can then either go home and continue working together online.
  • Or make plans to stay in a single location to carry on working.
  • There is no expectation that you would stay overnight at uni - plus the building will close!
  • But you are welcome to keep working!

What are participants expected to do during the event?

  • You will have 24 hours to build something, it can be anything you want!
  • We want you to have fun. And maybe learn something along the way.
  • Students will then post their projects on DEVPOST (details to follow), and the final projects will be judged by the industry sponsors.
  • We will also have masterclasses and workshops throughout the two days, which you can take part in.

Code of Conduct

Follow these and you'll be fine!

General

  • The Golden Rule: "Treat others how you would like to be treated!"
  • Hackabury is intended for anyone and everyone, no matter their gender, sexuality or beliefs.
  • This is a learning event! Be kind and help your teammates if you are able to.

Hackabury Specific

  • Don't try and steal code, sabotage others or attempt anything unsportsmanlike.
  • Be respectful of guest speakers. They have the right to not answer personal questions.
  • Most of the campus is unavailable and off limits during the event. Security will also be on site.
  • Anyone not following the Hackabury Code of Conduct will be disqualified from any prizes.
